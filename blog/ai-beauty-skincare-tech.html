What Is Red Light Therapy and Why Does It Work?

Before diving into the products, it's important to understand the science. Red light therapy (also called photobiomodulation or low-level light therapy) uses specific wavelengths of red and near-infrared light to penetrate the skin at different depths.

  • Red light (630–660 nm) — Targets the epidermis and upper dermis. Stimulates collagen production, reduces fine lines and wrinkles, improves skin tone and texture.
  • Near-infrared light (810–850 nm) — Penetrates deeper into the dermis and subcutaneous tissue. Reduces inflammation, accelerates wound healing, and stimulates cellular repair.
  • Blue light (415–470 nm) — Has antibacterial properties and is effective against acne-causing bacteria.
  • Amber/Yellow light (590 nm) — Soothes sensitive skin, reduces redness, and improves lymphatic circulation.

The science is well-established: hundreds of peer-reviewed studies support light therapy for skin rejuvenation, acne treatment, and wound healing. The key is using the correct wavelengths at the right power density — which is exactly what these AI-powered devices deliver.
